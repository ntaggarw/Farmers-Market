The Farmers' Market Project

An attempt to revive a better era of the food industry

Synopsis

Farmers Markets are not a necessarily new trend. In fact, the farmers' market derives from the original markeplace before the existence of supermarkets and large vendors. A farmers market is a markeplace in which many small vendors congregate in order to sell their goods together.

Since these events are usually hosted outside, environmental conditions have a large bearing over whether the market happens. This inconsistency causes a loss in business, because consumers do not have a reliable method of tracking whether the event is taking place.

Why This Matters

Being a resident of Chicago, I naturally found the supermarket as the most convenient place to shop. Everything was present in one place, there were labels on everything, and easy logistics when it came time to purchase my goods.

So why did I switch to the farmers market?

It always came down to a balance of trade-offs. As I got older, I attained or realized certain conditions I had that made the goods at a farmers market preferable to me. There is also a qualitative difference between the goods.

Characteristics Supermarket Farmers Market
Price Often set around a global average according to company policy. Priced slightly higher due to a lesss formal distribution of profits.
Quality Quality differs by product brand rather than store, but certain stores have higher expectations from their products before shelving them. Grown, raised or crafted through more traditional and meticulous methods, resulting in more personal care of the product and higher quality.
Quantity Official trade deals and contracts with hundreds of vendors. High quantity due to large supply chain(s). Medium to lower quantity due to stock being gained from few to no supply chains.
Safety and Regulations FDA regulates and approves most products in supermarkets and brands/companies do their own safety checks. Can afford to remove products from shelves if they are risky. Markets are hosted by an association of some sort that does rigorous safety checks. Products must still be approved by the FDA where applicable.
Availability Long hours every week for most. Once a week on average if whether allows, with either longer or shorter hours. there is a higher chance of being open on holidays.

I have severe allergies to pollen, which gets ever the more worse in the spring. I learned from a friend that local honey can reduce my symptoms; the only honey that worked was from a farmer in the market, who kept bees in the Chicagoland area. The honey was uniquely mixed so that it tasted better than any store-bought I tried. So when I can, I favor the Farmers market over the supermarket, even though it is a bit more walking and money.

Proposed Solution

Upon taking surveys and analyzing our research, we concluded that a combination of two simple methods would be enough to keep the status of the farmers market as lucid as possible.

We decided that the best approach would be to implement a website that keeps track of three things:

  1. Current dates and locations the farmers market will be happening.
  2. What each vendor attending has in stock.
  3. Requests for certain products or vendors.

There will also be an option to sign up for the second part of the solution: a weekly newsletter that will give all the above information succinctly to those who wish to simply stay informed.
