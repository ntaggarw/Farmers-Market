Research

We gathered our own data on top of secondary sources to help make our conclusions for how we would attempt this venture.

Our Research

One of the biggest obstacles we had to tackle was what form our solution would take. Since we knew relatively well what information needed to be transmitted, we just needed to decide method of reception. Thus, we put together a survey to ask that very question. It became apparent from the results that the majority wanted a website. But as we included another open-ended question on the survey about what other medium they would prefer, we realized that a newsletter would be more efficient.

I said a website because I thought it would be cool to see all the different foods, but I would honestly love to just see one of those spam emails that tell you everything so i don't have to check.

- Chris Slojkowski

Therefore, we concluded from our primary research that the best approach would be to implement both, to grab a bigger majority of the consumers.

Other Sources

We wanted to see what important information would we need to include in the newsletter/website, so we pulled up Farmers Markets: Consumer Trends, Preferences, and Characteristics as our source. This study provides an overview of attitudes, preferences and characteristics of consumers who shop at farmers’ markets. This helped give us an idea of what we wanted to include in our newsletter.

Then we aimed to determine the scope of the problem, and thus our audience. In combination with the previous journal, this journal allowed us a comprehensive view of who we were aiming to send our newsletters to. A Profile of Farmers' Market Consumers and the Perceived Advantages of Produce Sold at Farmers' Markets The demographics revealed that the majority of our consumers were more likely to be female, married, and post-graduate.
